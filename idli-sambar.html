Idli Sambar

A traditional South Indian breakfast that's light, healthy, and protein-rich

Prep Time

8 hours (including soaking)

Cook Time

30 minutes

Servings

4 people

About This Recipe

Idli Sambar is a classic South Indian breakfast combination that's not only delicious but also highly nutritious. The soft, fluffy idlis are made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, while the sambar is a flavorful lentil-based vegetable stew. This combination provides a perfect balance of carbohydrates, proteins, and essential nutrients.

Vegetarian Gluten-Free Protein-Rich

Ingredients

For Idli

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for grinding

For Sambar

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, pumpkin)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Making Idli Batter

  1. Wash and soak idli rice and urad dal separately for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind urad dal with fenugreek seeds to a smooth, fluffy batter.
  3. Grind rice to a slightly coarse batter.
  4. Mix both batters, add salt, and let it ferment overnight.
  5. Grease idli molds and pour the batter.
  6. Steam for 10-12 minutes until done.

Making Sambar

  1. Pressure cook toor dal with turmeric powder until soft.
  2. Cook vegetables in a separate pan until tender.
  3. Mash the cooked dal and add it to the vegetables.
  4. Add sambar powder, tamarind paste, and salt.
  5. Prepare tempering with oil, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
  6. Add the tempering to the sambar and simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Tips

  • Ensure proper fermentation of the idli batter for soft and fluffy idlis.
  • Use warm water while grinding the batter for better fermentation.
  • Don't overmix the batter after fermentation to maintain the airiness.
  • Adjust the consistency of sambar according to your preference.
  • You can add more vegetables to the sambar for extra nutrition.
