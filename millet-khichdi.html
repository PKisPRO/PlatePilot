Millet and Vegetable Khichdi

A nutritious one-pot meal combining protein-rich millets with seasonal vegetables and aromatic spices, perfect for a healthy and balanced diet.

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

30 minutes

Servings

4 people

Description

This Millet and Vegetable Khichdi is a modern twist on the traditional Indian comfort food. It combines three types of millets (foxtail, pearl, and finger millet) with seasonal vegetables and aromatic spices. Rich in protein, fiber, and essential minerals, this dish is not only delicious but also highly nutritious. The combination of different millets provides a complete amino acid profile, making it an excellent choice for vegetarians and health-conscious individuals.

Vegetarian Gluten-Free High Protein High Fiber

Ingredients

For the Khichdi:

  • 1/2 cup foxtail millet
  • 1/2 cup pearl millet
  • 1/2 cup finger millet
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, cauliflower)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Spice Mix:

  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 whole black peppercorns
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Preparation:

  1. Wash all millets and moong dal together until the water runs clear. Soak for 30 minutes.
  2. Chop all vegetables into uniform sizes.
  3. Prepare the spice mix by combining all dry spices.

Cooking:

  1. Heat ghee/oil in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  2. Add bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and peppercorns. Saut√© for 30 seconds.
  3. Add onions, ginger, and garlic. Saut√© until onions are translucent.
  4. Add tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add all dry spices and mix well.
  6. Add the soaked millets and dal, along with vegetables.
  7. Add 4 cups of water and salt. Mix well.
  8. Close the pressure cooker and cook for 3-4 whistles.
  9. Let the pressure release naturally.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Tips for Perfect Millet Khichdi

  • Soaking the millets helps in better digestion and faster cooking.
  • You can adjust the water quantity based on your preferred consistency.
  • For a richer taste, you can add a dollop of ghee before serving.
  • This dish can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • You can customize the vegetables based on seasonal availability.
